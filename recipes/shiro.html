Shiro

Shiro is a traditional Ethiopian dish made from roasted chickpea flour. It is a flavorful and protein-rich stew that is often served with injera, a spongy Ethiopian flatbread. Shiro is a staple in Ethiopian cuisine, loved for its simplicity and rich taste.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpea flour (Shiro powder)
  • 2 tablespoons berbere (Ethiopian spice blend)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cups water (adjust for desired consistency)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and cook until soft and golden.
  3. Stir in the garlic and ginger, cooking for another minute.
  4. Add the berbere spice and mix well with the onion mixture.
  5. Gradually add the chickpea flour while stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  6. Slowly pour in the water, continuing to stir until the mixture thickens.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and let the shiro simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Add salt to taste and adjust the water if necessary to achieve the desired consistency.
  9. Serve hot with injera or rice.
